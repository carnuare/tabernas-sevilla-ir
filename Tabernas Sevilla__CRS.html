1 Introduction

Tabernas Sevilla is a well-known chain of restaurants in Seville that has been around since 2001. There are four establishments across the capital, which are usually visited by young adults and families. Its specialty are sevillian tapas and dishes. They also have a very large wine menu

Our system fulfils three fundamental functions for Tabernas Sevilla's business model. It is based on a web page that allows the ordering of commands on-line. Using a computerized web menu, users can select which dishes from Tabernas Sevilla's extensive menu they wish to order and which of the 4 restaurants available throughout Seville to collect them from, giving them an estimated time to collect the order on site or selecting delivery to the previously specified address.

In addition, it has the functionality implemented to manage the capacity of the bar's customers, being able to make table reservations in advance by agreeing on a time, and if desired, also agreeing on the dishes to be consumed. In this way, the client does not have to wait for his dishes when he arrives at the bar but can enjoy them in the best possible way. This helps to solve one of the basic problems of the business, the management of the waiting list, which is currently done by hand by the waiters.

2 Information about the domain of the problem

2.1 Introduction to the problem domain

The company for which the creation of our system is intended is called Tabernas Sevilla. This chain of restaurants with 4 establishments in Seville has been serving tapas and wine since 2001.

These restaurants work very well both for regular customers and for passers-by, so we decided to provide new distribution methods and make the existing ones more efficient in order to allow this company to sell more products.

Tabernas Sevilla already has a website that allows the reservation of tables with or without a menu selection beforehand. We intend to add to the website the possibility for customers to have their meals delivered wherever they want around the restaurants. We also plan to add a take away system to avoid waiting for customers. These two systems will make it possible to sell products without being limited by the capacity of the different restaurants of Tabernas Sevilla.

In addition to these new sales methods, we are going to implement a social media involvement system consisting of a review system and others in order to inform the customer and, as its name suggests, involve him in the life of Tabernas Sevilla.

Finally, we have decided to improve the management of the waiting list by allowing us to inform the customer of how much time he or she has left before being able to settle in the restaurant.

All these actions can be carried out via the website once the customer has registered and logged in to their profile.

2.2 Glossary of terms of the problem domain

Bookings:

Bookings at Tabernas Sevilla can be made either by phone or directly through their website. These are only available for groups of 6 to 20 people and are available until 2:30 pm for lunch and 9:30 pm for dinner. Reservations are not made on the terrace so that there is always room for passers-by.

Once the reservation is made, an email will be sent to the customer to pay 25% of the price if he chose his menu during the reservation or 3 euros if he will order on the spot to validate the reservation. If the customer has not chosen a menu during the reservation, then he will not be able to order tapa unless it is the only format of the desired dish.

Customers, Diner:

Customers of Tabernas Sevilla are mostly families and young adults. We noticed that there were very few children when we went there.

Delivery:

Our site will offer an online ordering system that can then be either delivered or picked up directly at the desired restaurant. In addition, the details of the order will be available as well as the invoice.

The delivery system will display in real time the status of the order with an estimated time of arrival.

An estimated time for order pickup will also be provided.

Establishments, Taverns:

Tabernas Sevilla has four restaurants in different areas of the city of Seville, which in order of their opening are in Porvenir, Torneo, Nervión and Arenal.
These establishments all operate independently, however, they all offer the same menu, allowing customers to enjoy the same products regardless of the restaurant of this brand. In addition, all its establishments have a bar and a room, only the layout of these two elements and their location in the city change.

Figure 3: Taberna Arenal

Owner:

Tabernas Sevilla's manager is named Pablo Arenas. He is half owner of this business.

Review:

Our site will be equipped with a rating system that will allow you to rate and leave comments on the different dishes offered by this restaurant. Indeed, logged users will be able to give a rating from 0 to 5 stars and write a comment to support their rating.

Thus, customers and restaurant staff will have the opportunity to be informed of the success of a dish and have more details than a rating thanks to the comments.

Tapas:

Tapas are small salty Spanish dishes, usually served with drinks in a bar.Tabernas Sevilla sells 60 different tapas classified in 8 categories which are dessert, meat, own kitchen, salad, omelettes, bar, boards, cheese and cold meats which prices vary between 2 and 18 euros depending on the desired dish and its quantity.

These dishes are offered in 3 different quantities, in tapa, half ration and full ration, however, the tapas portions are only available by reservation if the customer indicates these dishes, otherwise, only the dishes only offered in tapas will be available once on site.

They are all available at this address: https://www.arengalia.es/carta-de-tapas

Figure 4: A picture of a dish

User profile:

The client will be able to connect to the Tabernas Sevilla website in order to access different functionalities such as delivery or take away of meals.
In addition, they will be able to give their opinion on the different dishes ordered through their profile and will be able to access the history of their orders in order to find a particular dish.

Waiting list:

The waiting list is independent at each Tabernas Sevilla restaurant. It is managed directly by the waiters and the restaurant manager.

It is for the moment very rudimentary but is intended to be computerized in order to allow a less long waiting time for customers and a better management for the staff.

Website:
The client will be able to connect to the Tabernas Sevilla website in order to access different functionalities such as delivery or take away of meals.

In addition, they will be able to give their opinion on the different dishes ordered through their profile and will be able to access the history of their orders in order to find a particular dish.

Wines:

Tabernas Sevilla offers 5 types of wines which are the generous, cellar, rosé, white and red for a total of 41 different wines to date.

These wines are offered in bottles whose price starts at 10 euros and can go up to 35 euros but some of them are also available in glass from 1.8 euros to 2.6 euros.

Finally, the customer can ask the waiter and access the history of the wines of the week of this brand, knowing that Tabernas Sevilla offering its wines of the week can be unique to one of the 4 restaurants or common to all.

All of them can be find at this adress : https://www.arengalia.es/carta-de-vinos

Working stations:

A piece of hardware that allows the waiter to assign the orders to a virtual table, and when the customer asks for the bill, the program will generate a bill according to the assigned food.

Working stations can be found in any of the establihsments, for each of them there is one static monitor for the bar, and a portable PDA for every waiter. Working stations use an application called BDP-NET software (see figures 8 and 9).

3 Description of the current situation

Currently, the business is going through challenging times. The pandemic that we live in makes it hard for the hostelry sector to succeed and reach their business goals. However, Tabernas Sevilla is financially stable and mature enough to resist these challenges.

3.1 Pros and cons of the current situation

3.1.1 Strengths of the current situation

01 - Maturity: - The main advantage of Tabernas Sevilla is the age of the business. Since they started in 2001, they have had enough time to fix all the problems that have appeared during the business lifetime and to gain a good reputation in Seville.

02 - No issues: - There are no significant issues to solve now, and that makes us able to make new improvements.

3.1.2 Weaknesses of the current situation

01 - Covid19: - The ongoing pandemic makes it difficult to invest in new features and techniques.

02 - Complex booking:

  • The current process to book a table in a restaurant is complex.

03 - No social media profiles:

  • There is no social media involvement to promote the business.

3.2 Models of the processes of the current business

3.2.1 Description of the actors of the current business

  • Owner: Our client, who oversees the general management of the 4 restaurants and decision making of the business. The owner contacts and deals with providers and selects the menu for the restaurants. He delegates his functions to the foreman.

  • Foremans: Skilled person with experience who is in charge of an establishment on behalf of the owner and also watches over a group of workers.

  • Waiters: Person in charge of taking commands and bringing dishes to the diner.

  • Chefs: Person in charge of preparing and cooking food for the diners.

  • Diners: Customer who comes to the business to consume the services we offer.

3.2.2 Description of the processes of the current business

[P-01], Reservation: To make a reservation, the client can call the number of the restaurant or fill a form to gather basic information about the reservation. Then, a foreman will send an email to the customer to pay the 25% of the price of the selected menu, or 3 euros per diner if there is no selected menu (in this case no tapas will be served, only full sized dishes, unless the only size of the dish is a tapa). This money will be refunded once the diners finish their meal.

[P-02], Customer enters into a tavern protocol: When a diner enters the tavern, a waiter must ask them if they would like to take a seat, and then ask them what they would like to drink. Then, after the diner or diners have decided what they would like to eat, the waiter will write it down in their working station, and immediately the chefs will start cooking the ordered dishes. Once the food is ready, the chefs will put the dishes in the kitchen window and the waiter will move it to the diners table. When a diner asks for the bill, the waiter will print it with the electronic command and put it in the diners table, wait for them to pay with cash or credit card and say goodbye to them. This process includes [P-04].

[P-03], Waiting list process: When a diner enters the tavern and the capacity of it is full, a waiter will ask them if they want to be in the waiting list. If they do, the waiter will write the name and phone number of the diner in a paper. When a table is free, the waiter must call the diner to let them know that they can come already.

[P-04], Order: A customer orders food in a tavern and the waiter will write it down in their portable working station. The chef receives the order and cheks if there are all the ingredients necessary, if there aren't they will put on a reminder to call for the provider later at the end of the day, if there are all the ingredients necessary, the chefs will start cooking and when it's done the food will be forwarded to the diner through the waiter.

4.2 Models of the processes of the business to be developed

4.2.1 Description of the actors of the business to be developed

The business actors will remain the same as the current ones, with the novelty of User.

  • User: Customer who has previously registered on the website to order food and/or to make a reservation

4.2.2 Description of the processes of the business to be developed

  • Place an order: after you have registered on the website and logged in, fill in a form with the food you want to order and the time you want to pick it up locally. You can also select the option to have the order delivered to a home address.

  • Reserve a table: after you have registered on the website and logged in, you fill in a form specifying which of the four venues you want to book, at what time you want to reserve a table and, optionally, you can choose what dishes you are going to eat so that when you arrive at the venue, those dishes are already prepared and you don't have to wait too long. You cannot book a table less than one hour in advance

  • Waiting list management: after you have registered on the website and logged in, you can see the estimated waiting time based on the number of people currently in the venue and the bookings already selected.

  • Rate dishes: after you have registered on the website, logged in and scanned the invoice for the products consumed, you can rate them. This will form a ranking in which the most ordered dishes and the best rated dishes will participate.

5 Description of the subsystems to develop

There will only be a system to develop, that is a website for all to use. This website will use a database to store all the information regarding the users, accounts and the products that are being sold.

The website will be deployed using a tool like google engine, where you have to pay an amount of money to keep it running, and the database will be stored in the owner's computer, so he can have easy access.

Results

The day of the meeting we went to the offices of our client, Pablo Arenas. We met around 5pm and the meeting lasted about an hour and a half.
He made it clear to us that his main objective is to sell, every improvement or new functionality that we create does not matter if he cannot sell more than now.
First, we listened to our client how his company was working and the computerized system he was using ("BDP" it's the software's name) to better understand his business model and how we could improve it or design new functionalities. He explained in depth how the restaurant business works. He told us that they have 4 establishments in Seville, and each of them has a manager, some waiters and some cooks (the number depends on the location). In these taverns you can find typical spanish tapas, such as croquetas, a variety of meats, san jacobos, cheese, iberic ham? At the end of the day, if some ingredients goes out of stock, the manager of the establishment notifies Pablo Arenas and he proceeds make an order to the providers. We also asked him some questions about the business and the way he manages it, for example:

Do you think your business lacks automation?

  • "I think we have a pretty computerized business".
    What are you thinking about to improve your business?
  • "Some time ago we thought of a system to manage waitlists, but we gave it up for lack of time to manage it. So I listen to your proposals".
    How does the booking system works?
  • "If you call the telephone number of the tavern or you make a reservation through the web, you have to indicate the number of diners, the time of arival, and you can (optional) specify a menu if you want to have it served quickly when you get here. The cost of reservation is 3 euros per diner, and this ammount will be refunded when you pay before leaving the tavern."

    Finally, we chose between the customer and ourselves what we thought was best for his business, we thought four possible solutions:
    o The "take away" and "delivery" system (to fulfil the main goal: sell more).
    o Improvement of the restaurant's waitlist management:
    o Making possible the online booking without going to the restaurant.
    o Creating a software that manages face-to-face waiting list automatically.

Results

We had to make this second meeting via Skype due to the ongoing situation caused by the pandemic.

The goal of this meeting was to ask a few doubts we had about the bussiness and to seek approval of our client.

We showed him all of the work we have done so far, what interested him the most was the mockups, and he was very impressed with the result. He liked it overall, but he didn't really understood the purpose of the reviews since it can damage the reputation of the bussiness.

Here are the questions we asked him:

How long can you book in advance?

  • "You can make a reservation with a maximum of 30 days in advance."

    And what are the cancellation policies?
  • "If you cancell your reservation with a margin of 48 hours or more, you can have your money back, if you cancel it within 48 hours before the reservation we won't make a refund of the money you paid for the booking. We do this to avoid ghost clients."

    Can you book outside the tavern or only inside?
  • "Both, inside and outside."

    How do you feel about making promotions like the ones you can find in McDonalds and other fast food chains?
  • "Well, I haven't seen any bar or restaurant make these kind of offers, but maybe it could work -to have a point system for every time you order something- to keep the clients coming."

    After that we talked about the bussiness in these difficult times of the pandemic. Pablo Arenas, our client, told us that during this time they have inmplemented a take-away system (stealing our idea), and they now offer meriendas in their taverns. After all they can handle the situation, if could be worse he told us. He hopes that when all of this is over they can start making the profit they made before the pandemic.
